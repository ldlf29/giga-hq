# GIGATHON 1 - Gigaverse Hackathon (June ‘26)

Gigaverse Vibe Coding Hackathon is a 10-day long online hackathon where builders create clients, mods, mini games, experiences, and utilities around Gigaverse and Gigling Racing, our new pet racing game.

Builders can work solo or in small teams and can use AI-assisted development (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, etc.) to rapidly prototype ideas.

#### The goal is to expand the Gigaverse ecosystem with creative player tools, community experiences, developer utilities, and educational content. Prizes are distributed across multiple categories to encourage diverse approaches.

---

## 🎯 Core Concept

- **Vibe coding friendly**: Builders can use AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, etc.)
- **Gigaverse-centric**: Everything should plug into or enhance Gigaverse
- **API-driven**: We provide docs, endpoints, sample data, and references
- **Open & global**: Solo builders or small teams
- **Build anything useful** for the game, players, creators, community, etc.
- **Total Duration:** 10 days

---

## 🛠️ What Can Be Built

Builders can create **anything useful** for the players and community as a whole — anything that plugs into or enhances Gigling Racing or the Gigaverse ecosystem. Use the categories below as inspiration (non-exhaustive).

### 1. Community & Social

**Focus:** Build social layers & community engagement.

Examples:

- Live race spectator UI with sabotage notifications
- Pet/owner profiles with names, avatars, and win histories
- Faction vs faction live scoreboards & battles
- Discord/Telegram bots for race updates & alerts
- Rivalry / Ally tracking (who boosts/sabotages whom most)

### 2. Educational & Onboarding

**Focus:** Teach new & existing players how to play effectively.

Examples:

- Interactive tutorials & game mechanics explainers
- Strategy guides (betting, item usage, faction bonuses)
- Walkthroughs for onboarding new players
- Live data-driven economics tutorials

### 3. Creative

**Focus:** Bring out your inner artist.

Examples:

- Lore & worldbuilding (pet origins, faction stories)
- Pet name/identity generator with lore
- Generative pixel art for custom pet skins
- 3D race visualization or ASCII art live feeds
- Music, audio cues, or immersive UX experiments

### 4. Novel & Experimental

**Focus:** Explore novel ideas and experiences.

Examples:

- Prediction markets for Gigling races
- Mini games using Gigaverse game items
- Full reskinned version of Gigling Racing using the underlying contracts
- Localized language version of Gigling Racing
- Comprehensive tournament creator and manager suite
- Gigling breeding manager

### 5. Player Tools & Analytics

**Focus:** Help players understand, strategize, and optimize gameplay.

Examples:

- Pet stat dashboards & historical race analysis
- Calculators & odds estimators
- Item efficiency tools (when to boost/sabotage)
- Win-rate trackers by faction/rarity
- Leaderboards (pet, player, faction-based)

### 6. Developer & Integration Utilities

**Focus:** Build tools for developers & bot builders.

Examples:

- TypeScript/Python SDK for race data & events
- Live event streaming engine for websockets/webhooks
- Contract interaction UI (create race, enter race, place bets onchain)
- Indexed race history & real-time data explorer

## ⚖️ Judging Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on the following criteria, with special preference for Gigling Racing compared to the overall Gigaverse Ecosystem.

| Criteria | Description | Weightage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Execution | Does it work? Is it usable & polished? Code quality & UX matter. | 30% |
| Creativity | Novel ideas, clever execution, unexpected angle. Bonus: combining multiple domains. | 25% |
| Usefulness | Does this genuinely help players, builders, or the ecosystem? Is it solving a real problem? | 20% |
| Gigaverse Alignment | Strong integration with Gigaverse ecosystem & vision. Special emphasis and preference for Gigling Racing. | 20% |
| Potential | Could this evolve into a long-term tool? | 5% |

## 📌 Important Notes & Caveats

> This is our first hackathon, and more than anything we want it to be defined by participation, a lighthearted spirit, and good fun. Please join in that spirit. And of course, share your feedback & ideas on how to make future ones even better.
> 

> We also know how personal it can feel to have your work judged. Evaluating qualitative factors like creativity and usefulness inevitably involves some subjectivity, and there will likely be excellent projects, built with real effort, that don’t win their category. If that’s you, please know your participation is valued every bit as much as the winners’.
> 

> Finally, we reserve the right not to highlight or feature any submission for any reason — in particular, where there is any security concern or blindspot.
